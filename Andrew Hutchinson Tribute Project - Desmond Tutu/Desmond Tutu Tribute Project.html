Archbishop Desmond Tutu - A Tribute

"My humanity is bound up in yours, for we can only be human together".

Some Background on the Archbishop:

The first thing you have to understand about Archbishop Desmond Tutu, is that he is hero in his own right. Why?

Well, in no small manner of saying, he was awarded the Nobel Prize in recognition of the following:

"the courage and heroism shown by black South Africans in their time with the peaceful method of struggle against apartheid."

On October 7th 1931, Desmond Tutu was born in Klerksdorp, in the South African state of Transvaal. The family moved to Johannesburg when he was 12, and he attended Johannesburg Bantu High School.

Although he had planned to become a physician, his parents could not afford to send him to medical school.

In 1954, Desmond Tutu Graduates from the University of South Africa.

When the government ordained a deliberately inferior system of education for black students, Desmond Tutu refused to cooperate. He could no longer work as a teacher, but he was determined to do something to improve the life of his disenfranchised people. On the advice of his bishop, he began to study for the Anglican priesthood.

In 1960, Desmond Tutu is ordained as a priestin the Anglican church.

Desmond Tutu goes on to fight the opression of Aparthied that grips the country, and never gives up on his mission of peace between all.

Testemony to his unwaivering will, here is a list of some memorable achievements:

  • 1975: Becomes the first black dean in Johannesburg
  • 1976: Becomes the Bishop of Lesotho
  • 1984: Desmond Tutu is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1985: Beceomes the first Bishop of Johannesburg
  • 1986: He is awarded the Albert Schweitzer Prize for Humanitarianism
  • 1999: He is awarded the Sydney Peace Prize
  • 2007: He is awarded the Gandhi Peace Prize
  • 2008: He is awarded the J. William Fulbright Prize for International Understanding
  • 2009: He is awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom (2009)

A final word by the Archbishop, which, in my opinion, encapsulates the man quite aptly:

"Without forgiveness, there's no future."
